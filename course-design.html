About This Course

What This Course Covers

The Graphic Design Essentials course is a complete, hands-on program designed to take you from a complete beginner to a confident, portfolio-ready designer. You will learn not just how to use design tools, but how to think visually and solve real communication problems through design.

The curriculum is built around real design briefs — logo projects, brand identity systems, social media campaigns, print layouts, and digital UI elements. You will work like a professional designer from day one, learning by doing across every major software tool used in the industry.

By graduation, you will have a polished portfolio of diverse design work, a deep understanding of color theory, typography, and visual hierarchy, and the confidence to take on freelance clients or apply for junior designer roles at agencies and studios.

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need to buy expensive software like Adobe?
Adobe offers a 7-day free trial for all Creative Cloud apps, which is enough to get started. We also cover Figma, which is completely free. We guide you through the most cost-effective ways to access the tools, including student discounts and free alternatives for certain tasks.
Do I need any artistic talent to take this course?
Absolutely not. Graphic design is a learnable skill, not an innate talent. We teach you design thinking from scratch. Some of our best students had no artistic background at all — they just applied the principles consistently and produced outstanding work.
What computer specs do I need?
Any modern laptop or desktop with at least 8GB RAM will work. Both Windows and Mac are supported. We recommend 16GB RAM for smoother Photoshop performance, but 8GB is enough for most of the course. We provide a full setup checklist at the start.
Will I get feedback on my design projects?
Yes. All major projects are submitted for critique. You will receive structured feedback from Sara and the community. Design critique is one of the fastest ways to improve, and we take it seriously. Your portfolio projects go through multiple review rounds before finalization.
Is this course more focused on digital or print design?
Both. We cover the full spectrum — logo design, brand identity, print layouts, social media content, UI design in Figma, and digital advertising. This breadth is intentional so you can serve a wide variety of clients and roles as a designer.
Can I become a freelance designer after this course?
Definitely. The final module is specifically dedicated to freelancing — pricing, client management, proposal writing, and portfolio presentation. Many of our graduates begin taking paid freelance projects before they even complete the course.
